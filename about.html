Marvel Studios, LLC (originally known as Marvel Films from 1993 to 1996) is an American film and television production company. Marvel Studios is the creator of the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU), a media franchise and shared universe of films and television series produced by the studio, based on characters that appear in Marvel Comics publications. The studio was founded by Avi Arad as part of Marvel Entertainment and has been led by producer Kevin Feige, who serves as its president, since 2007. The studio originally licensed the film rights to several Marvel characters before beginning to produce its own films in 2004, and has since regained many of those rights. It has been owned by the Walt Disney Company since 2009, and has been a subsidiary of Walt Disney Studios since August 2015, where it is part of Disney Entertainment. Walt Disney Studios Motion Pictures has distributed most of the studio's films since The Avengers (2012).

Since 2008, Marvel Studios has released 33 films within the MCU, from Iron Man (2008) to The Marvels (2023); ten television series since 2021, from WandaVision (2021) to Echo (2024); and two television specials: Werewolf by Night (2022) and The Guardians of the Galaxy Holiday Special (2022). Since 2024, Marvel Studios has used "Marvel Television" and "Marvel Animation" banners to release their television and animated projects, respectively.

STAN LEE

Stan Lee, born Stanley Martin Lieber on December 28, 1922, was a legendary American comic book writer, editor, and publisher. He created many of the most iconic Marvel characters of all time. As the former president and chairman of Marvel Comics, Lee played a pivotal role in establishing the Marvel Universe and turning it into a global entertainment powerhouse. He is best known for co-creating beloved superheroes such as Spider-Man, Iron Man, the Hulk, Thor, the X-Men, and the Fantastic Four. Lee's innovative storytelling, relatable characters, and unique comic book style made him a revered figure in popular culture. Through his work, he inspired generations of fans and creators, cementing his legacy as one of the most influential figures in the history of comic books and the entertainment industry. Lee's work has left an indelible mark on popular culture. Beyond his contributions to comics, Lee was a beloved figure, often making cameo appearances in Marvel films, endearing himself to generations of fans. He passed away on November 12, 2018, leaving behind a legacy that continues to inspire and entertain.

KEVIN FEIGE

Kevin Feige is a renowned American film producer and the mastermind behind the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU). Born on June 2, 1973, in Boston, Massachusetts, Feige's career in film began after he graduated from the University of Southern California's School of Cinematic Arts. He started as an intern for producer Lauren Shuler Donner, where he worked on the first "X-Men" film in 2000. Recognizing his potential, Marvel Studios hired him in 2000, and he quickly rose through the ranks to become president of production in 2007.Feige's innovative vision led to the creation of the MCU, a series of interconnected films that began with "Iron Man" in 2008. This pioneering concept transformed the superhero genre, creating a cohesive universe that allowed characters and storylines to cross over between films. Under his leadership, Marvel Studios has produced a string of critically acclaimed and commercially successful films. As President of Marvel Studios, Feige has overseen the production and release of over 20 blockbuster superhero films since 2008, starting with Iron Man. Through his strategic vision and collaborative leadership, Feige has shepherded the interconnected MCU into a pop culture phenomenon, bringing iconic Marvel characters like Spider-Man, Captain America, and Black Panther to life on the big screen. His ability to balance creative storytelling with commercial success has transformed Marvel into a powerhouse, making Feige one of the most influential producers in Hollywood today.
